How to read the map

The semiconductor industry is a chain of dependencies. A company can have strong demand and still capture little value; another can sit one layer upstream and own the scarce tool every winner must buy.

This map separates strategic position from stock opportunity. The highest-quality company is not automatically the best investment if the market already knows the story.

Scoring logic

Opportunity score is a weighted research heuristic: 30% strategic position, 25% earnings setup, 25% valuation discrepancy and 20% convexity. It is not a price target and should not be treated as a mechanical buy signal.

Bottleneck score is shown separately because a monopoly-like asset can still be a poor stock at the wrong valuation, while a cyclical company with a lower moat can offer a better setup at the right point in the cycle.
